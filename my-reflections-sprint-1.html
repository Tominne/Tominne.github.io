What does my research say about 'Flipped Learning' and how does it relate to our study?
FLipped learning is switching from lecture based class time approach to a group discussion/interactive/problem solving/activity based class time with presentations and lectures being homework does prior to this class period. This relates to us for a few reasons, as half our learning is based of problem solving alonside others, and what better time to practise that than when we are all together rather than simply absorbing presented material during this time instead of prior. Whilst this learning and teaching method sounds more satisfying and ideal more many, it is ofcourse another step toward better learning from a problematic one, and therefore should not be looked at as a solution, but merely a step in the problem solving that is how to teach and learn well. We are always making progress and should acknowledge that one style rarely suites all and look at each person as a whole being with their own valid needs and having the 'problem solving skills' and flexibility to adjust for differing needs is always going to be more helpful the one 'golden style' of teaching and learning./details>
